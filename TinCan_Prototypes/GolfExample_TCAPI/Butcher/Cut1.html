Making Your First Cuts

Cross-Cuts

Cross-cuts are when you cut a board across the grain. In this step, I start by cutting the wood into 2 foot long pieces to be more manageable to handle. I chose 2 feet because I want to my butcher block to be roughly 2 feet long. This can be as big or as small as you want.

Rip

Ripping your wood means to splice it down the middle, with the grain. I now rip the boards into 2 inch strips. This will make 2 inch squares as my pattern for the top of the board.
