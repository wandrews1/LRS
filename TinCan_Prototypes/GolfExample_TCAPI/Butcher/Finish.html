Finishing Touches

Heat Branding Iron

If you have a branding iron for your logo or slogan, you can apply it to your butcher block now. Most hardwoods will accept a proper brand once the brand is higher than 500 degrees Fahrenheit.

Applying Your Brand

Ensure, while pressing down evenly with your brand, you do not move the brand or else your mark will look sloppy. Try to be perfectly still. Hold firm pressure straight down for approximately 10 seconds.

Measure for Feet

With your square, mark 1.25 inches in from the corners with a pencil. With the two sides measured at an even distance, this will give a symmetrical look for all four feet.

Attach Feet

Attaching rubber feet not only keeps the butcher block in place while in use, but it also prevents the butcher block from sitting in a puddle of water, preventing the block from warping.

Applying the Finish

It is impairative that you use a food-grade natural oil on this. Here, I used a mixture of food-grade mineral oil and beeswax. Do not use any cooking oils as they will eventually spoil thus rendering your butcher block useless.

Maintenance

  • Do not use bleach
  • Do not soak
  • For stains, use white vinegar
  • Reapply mineral oil at least once per month
