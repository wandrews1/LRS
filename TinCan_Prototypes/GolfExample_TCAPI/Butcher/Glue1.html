Glue-Up 1

Turn Blocks on Side

Turn all the strips on their sides, except for the last one. This is the end peice, so it's not going to get any glue.

Pour Glue

Pour the glue on all the peices you turned on their side. Don't be frugal. Here, I am using Titebond III because it is waterproof.

Spread Glue

Spread the glue evenly. Wood glue works best when applied in thin lines. I have a Rockler Glue Spreader and it spreads the glue into lines perfectly. If you don't have a brush, you can easily use your hand. The glue will easily peel off, harmlessly.

Turn Blocks Back on Bottom

Turn the strips back on their bottoms and press them against one another. Make sure the edges line up to minimize size loss in later steps.

Clamps

Apply two heavy-duty grade clamps. I prefer to use clamps that exceed 1000 lbs of force. This ensures a tight bond. Before you tighten all the way, make sure all edges are properly aligned. This will be your last chance to ensure this.

More Clamps

After everything is properly aligned, add more clamps. The more the better. Wait at least 6 hours before taking the clamps off.
