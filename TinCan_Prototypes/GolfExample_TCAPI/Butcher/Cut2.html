Cuts, Again

Planer

Now that your wood is dry and you've taken off the clamps, run the block through the thickness planer again. This will make the board completely flat after glueing multiple pieces back together. Flip the board over and plane the other side as well.

Cross-Cuts

Using a cross-cut sled on the table saw, I cut 2 inch thick strips. Here, I chose 2 inches because I want my butcher block to be 2 inches thick (tall). Keep these in same order that you cut them. This will be important in the final look of the block because of end-grain uniformity.
